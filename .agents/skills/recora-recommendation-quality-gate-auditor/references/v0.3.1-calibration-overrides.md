# v0.3.1 Calibration Overrides

Use this reference after v0.3-real when auditing real Recora recommendation candidates.

v0.3.1 is calibrated to the actual review results below:

| Candidate ID | Decision | quality_score | Confidence | Risk | Evidence grade |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| `rec-23677594-brand-visibility-gap` | `auto_publish` | 84 | `medium` | `low` | `A-` |
| `rec-23677594-citation-evidence-review` | `hold` | 69 | `medium` | `medium` | `B-` |
| `rec-23677594-case-study-evidence-gap` | `hold` | 52 | `low` | `medium` | `C` |

## 1. Auto-Publish And Score Threshold Alignment

Do not require a rigid score of 85 for every `auto_publish` decision.

Use this rule:

- `auto_publish` is allowed at `quality_score >= 80` only when all publishability conditions are met.
- The candidate must have direct, traceable primary evidence.
- The primary claim must not depend on `NEEDS_VERIFICATION`.
- `risk_level` must be `low`.
- `evidence_grade` must be `A` or `A-`.
- Wording must stay inside the measured observation scope.
- The safe rewrite must include observed scope, trend language, and re-verification.

Calibration anchor:

- `rec-23677594-brand-visibility-gap` is `auto_publish` at `84`, not because 84 is automatically publishable, but because it has direct observation evidence, low risk, no citation-proof dependency, and scoped language.

Hold even above 80 when:

- the primary evidence is citation presence without claim support.
- source freshness, URL scope, or source-to-claim mapping is unresolved.
- wording overstates partial observations.
- the candidate has medium or high display risk.
- the safe rewrite lacks observed-scope or re-verification language.

## 2. Citation Count Is Not Claim Support

Treat citation rows and source support as separate evidence dimensions.

Citation rows can support:

- a citation URL was observed.
- a domain appeared in a measured answer.
- a citation review task is warranted.

Citation rows do not support by themselves:

- the cited source proves the AI answer.
- the cited source supports the candidate recommendation.
- the domain is authoritative.
- the source is current, official, or relevant.
- the client should use the URL as evidence.

When `supports_claim` is `unknown`, null, missing, or not mapped to the exact answer claim:

- mark claim support as `NEEDS_VERIFICATION`.
- cap claim-support confidence at `medium`.
- set `risk_level` to at least `medium` for client-facing use.
- use `hold` unless the candidate is only an internal citation-verification item.
- suppress any wording that says citation URLs prove the recommendation.

Calibration anchor:

- `rec-23677594-citation-evidence-review` has 26 citation rows, but all claim support is unknown. Its correct decision is `hold`, score `69`, risk `medium`, evidence grade `B-`.

Recommended flags:

- `citation_count_not_claim_support`
- `supports_claim_unknown`
- `source_to_claim_mapping_missing`
- `citation_verification_required`
- `url_presence_not_proof`

## 3. Single Observation, Seeded Prompt, And No Page-Gap Proof

Do not turn a one-row observation into a confirmed client finding.

Use `hold` when all are true:

- the candidate is useful as an internal verification item.
- the wording avoids a confirmed deficiency.
- the output asks the reviewer to verify site evidence or additional observations.

Use `suppress` when any are true:

- the candidate claims the site lacks a page or asset without page evidence.
- the candidate treats a brand-seeded prompt as organic AI visibility.
- the candidate claims competitor dominance without clean competitor measurement.
- the candidate guarantees AI citation, ranking, recommendation, traffic, conversion, or revenue.

Seeded prompt boundary:

- A brand-seeded prompt can support retrievability or answer-quality review for that named brand.
- It cannot support organic discovery, non-branded visibility, market consensus, or competitor dominance.

No page-gap proof boundary:

- An AI answer that says information was not found is not enough to prove the site lacks that information.
- A page-gap claim needs site inspection, crawl evidence, source ledger evidence, or repeated clean observations.

Calibration anchor:

- `rec-23677594-case-study-evidence-gap` is `hold`, score `52`, confidence `low`, risk `medium`, evidence grade `C` because it has one observation, one matched clue, a brand-seeded prompt, and no page-gap proof.

Recommended flags:

- `single_observation`
- `single_prompt_scope`
- `seeded_prompt`
- `organic_visibility_not_supported`
- `no_page_gap_proof`
- `client_finding_not_confirmed`

## 4. Evidence Grade Calibration

Use these evidence grades for the v0.3.1 patterns:

- `A`: direct, repeated measurement; low risk; current scope clear; primary claim fully supported.
- `A-`: direct measurement with limited sample or single-run scope, but safe wording and low risk.
- `B`: useful measured evidence with some unresolved verification.
- `B-`: measured presence of a signal, but primary claim support is incomplete or needs human review.
- `C`: weak but usable internal verification signal; low sample, seeded prompt, or no independent proof.
- `D`: unsafe, misleading, invented, contaminated, or disconnected from evidence.

## 5. Reviewer Comment Calibration

Use concise comments that distinguish the observed fact from the inference:

- Brand visibility: "Auto-publish with observed-scope wording. The evidence supports a measured visibility gap within this run, not total AI invisibility."
- Citation review: "Hold because citation URLs were observed but source-to-claim support is still unknown. URL presence is not proof."
- Case-study gap: "Hold as a verification item. One seeded observation and one clue do not prove a case-study or page gap."
