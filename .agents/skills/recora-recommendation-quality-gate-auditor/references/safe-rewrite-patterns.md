# Safe Rewrite Patterns From Actual Candidates

Use these rewrites for risky interpretations of the actual candidate wording.

## Brand Visibility Gap

Risky:

- "The brand is not visible in AI search."

Safe:

- "In this Recora observation set, the brand was not clearly shown in some tested questions. Treat this as a measured visibility gap within the tested query set."

Risky:

- "The client is losing to competitors."

Safe:

- "The observations suggest areas where competitor or category evidence should be reviewed. Confirm competitor visibility before making a competitive dominance claim."

## Citation Evidence Review

Risky:

- "These citation URLs prove the recommendation."

Safe:

- "The citation URLs were observed, but each source still needs review to confirm whether it supports the answer's claim."

Risky:

- "The cited domains are trustworthy sources."

Safe:

- "The cited domains are candidate sources for verification. Source type, ownership, and claim support must be checked before client-facing use."

## Case Study Evidence Gap

Risky:

- "The site lacks case studies."

Safe:

- "One observed answer suggested that proof or case-study information may need additional confirmation. Verify the site's case-study and adoption-evidence pages before publishing this as a finding."

Risky:

- "Adding case studies will make AI cite the client."

Safe:

- "Clear case studies may improve evidence readiness, but AI citation must be measured separately."

## Cross-Candidate Guardrails

- Replace "confirmed" with "observed in this run" when the evidence is run-limited.
- Replace "proves" with "suggests" when source-to-claim support is incomplete.
- Replace "will improve" with "may improve readiness" when impact has not been measured.
- Keep Recora metric caution whenever the output may be client-facing.
