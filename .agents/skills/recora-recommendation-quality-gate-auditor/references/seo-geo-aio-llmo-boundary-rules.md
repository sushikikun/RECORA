# SEO GEO AIO LLMO Boundary Rules

Use this reference to keep recommendation candidates technically correct and evidence-scoped.

## Expert Boundaries

- Google-oriented GEO/AIO work should be treated as SEO plus AI feature visibility, not magic separate optimization.
- Google guidance can support crawlability, indexability, snippet eligibility, useful content, and structured data boundaries when verified from current Google documentation.
- Structured data is a comprehension and eligibility aid, not a ranking or AI citation guarantee.
- AI crawler access is access-condition work, not inclusion, citation, or recommendation guarantee.
- GEO research supports visibility and attribution concepts, not commercial outcome guarantees.
- RAG research supports retrieval-grounding concepts, not universal platform behavior.
- Empirical AI search studies support separating SERP visibility from AI answer visibility.
- Current competitor visibility always needs actual measurement.

## Decision Implications

Use `auto_publish` only when the candidate:

- stays within these boundaries.
- ties the recommendation to direct measurement or verified official-source guidance.
- avoids guaranteed outcomes.
- uses client-safe wording.

Use `hold` when:

- the direction is sound but current platform behavior is unverified.
- source status or measurement context is incomplete.
- the candidate needs narrower wording.

Use `suppress` when:

- the candidate treats GEO/AIO as magic.
- the candidate says schema, crawler access, content format, or source additions guarantee AI citation.
- the candidate treats SERP ranking as proof of AI answer visibility.
- the candidate invents competitor visibility.

## Safe Boundary Phrases

- "This supports readiness, not guaranteed inclusion."
- "This is a Search and AI-feature visibility hypothesis that needs measurement."
- "Structured data can clarify page content but does not guarantee citation."
- "Crawler access can improve access conditions but does not guarantee recommendation."
- "Research supports the concept, not this client's outcome."
