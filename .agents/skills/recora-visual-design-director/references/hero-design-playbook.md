# Hero Design Playbook

Use this for Recora LP hero, above-the-fold, H1, CTA, first product mock, and sample-note decisions.

## BtoB SaaS Hero Job

The hero must answer:

- What is Recora?
- Who is it for?
- What business decision does it support?
- What can I see or do next?
- Can I trust the product surface?

The hero should not try to explain the entire GEO/AI search category. It should create enough clarity and confidence to continue.

## Recommended Hero Structure

```md
Header
Hero eyebrow or category label
H1
Short subcopy
CTA row
Small trust/scope note
Large product mock or report preview
Next section hint
```

## Japanese H1 Rules

- Desktop: 44-56px.
- Mobile: 30-36px.
- Line-height: 1.12-1.25 for H1.
- Prefer 1-2 lines desktop and 2-3 lines mobile.
- Break lines by semantic chunks, not by equal character count.
- Avoid long noun chains and excessive English acronyms in the H1.
- Do not make the H1 so heavy that it feels like a poster instead of SaaS UI.

Better H1 shape:

```md
AI検索で、選ばれる理由と
見落とされる理由を可視化する
```

Risky H1 shape:

```md
AI時代の次世代型GEO最適化プラットフォームで
検索体験を革新的にアップデート
```

## CTA Placement

- Primary CTA must be visible in the first viewport.
- Use one primary CTA and one quieter secondary action at most.
- Primary label must describe the real next step.
- If the form is not confirmed to start analysis automatically, avoid "分析を開始" and prefer safer wording such as "無料チェックを相談する" or "サンプルを見る".
- Keep CTA height around 44-52px, with enough horizontal padding and visible focus state.

## Sample Note Placement

- Put sample notes near the product mock, inside mock chrome, or under the CTA as a compact note.
- Good labels: "サンプル", "参考表示", "サンプル分析", "表示例".
- Avoid giant watermarks or warnings that make the product feel fake.
- Avoid hiding the label so much that sample data could be mistaken for real customer results.

## Center Hero vs Left/Right Hero

Use centered hero when:

- The product mock can sit directly below the copy and become the visual anchor.
- The message is short and needs a calm premium feel.
- Mobile should preserve the same reading order.

Use left/right hero when:

- The right side contains a large, detailed mock with real visual weight.
- The copy column is not oversized.
- The mock is not squeezed below 50 percent of the visual importance.

Avoid left/right hero when:

- The right mock is small, empty, or decorative.
- The left H1 becomes a giant text block.
- The layout creates a generic SaaS-template split.

## SE Ranking-Style Hero Checklist

Use this only as a quality direction, not a copy target:

- White or near-white background.
- Calm header and strong first viewport alignment.
- H1 is confident but not oversized.
- Product screen is large, polished, and central to persuasion.
- CTA is obvious, deep colored, and not noisy.
- Supporting details are compact.
- Product mock contains enough UI density to feel real.
- Next section begins within or just below the first viewport.

## Hero Anti-Patterns

- Abstract AI orb or gradient as the main visual.
- Product mock hidden in a small card.
- H1 uses "AI-powered", "next-generation", or "all-in-one" without proof.
- Multiple CTAs with equal visual weight.
- First viewport ends with no product evidence.
- Unsupported provider badges in the hero.
- Sample note missing when sample values appear.
