# Reference Site Patterns

Use these as directional pattern references only. Do not copy text, images, CSS, HTML, logos, exact layouts, or assets from reference sites. If a task requires current reference-site comparison, inspect current pages or user-provided screenshots before making specific claims; otherwise treat the notes below as stable design direction.

## SE Ranking

Reference qualities to borrow:

- White-first SaaS surface with generous but controlled spacing.
- Hero that feels ordered and product-forward.
- Strong product screen presentation rather than decorative AI art.
- Card density that mixes text with UI fragments, metrics, mini charts, and tables.
- Clear primary CTA and calmer secondary actions.
- FAQ and final CTA that feel integrated rather than pasted on.

Do not copy:

- Their copy, screenshots, illustrations, brand styling, layout exactness, icon system, or asset treatment.

## Peec

Reference qualities to borrow:

- Simple SaaS LP structure with fewer, stronger messages.
- Large product preview as the persuasion center.
- Metrics and indicators shown selectively rather than flooding the page.
- Calm section rhythm and clear CTA hierarchy.

Do not copy:

- Product claims, screenshots, wording, visual identity, or component styling.

## Gumshoe

Reference qualities to borrow:

- AI answer analysis framed by persona, buying criteria, and decision context.
- Visualizing what different buyer types see or value.
- Making competitive comparison feel structured rather than noisy.
- Showing qualitative AI-answer insights alongside compact scoring or ranking.

Do not copy:

- Their terminology, copy blocks, graphics, data examples, or branded framework names.

## Rankscale

Reference qualities to borrow:

- Product-feeling motion or progressive reveal in the hero.
- Feature-stack storytelling that shows capabilities as a system.
- Energetic but controlled product presentation.
- Interactive or animated cues that reinforce product confidence.

Do not copy:

- Animation details, exact composition, code, CSS, brand elements, or visual assets.

## Recora Adaptation Rules

- Recora stays green-based, Japanese, BtoB, refined, and trustworthy.
- Prefer white background and product clarity over heavy gradients or spectacle.
- Keep motion subtle if used. Motion should clarify hierarchy or product flow, not distract.
- Use mini UI and sample report fragments to create density instead of adding many generic cards.
- Preserve product truth. Reference-site polish must not become unverified feature claims.
