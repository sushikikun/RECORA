# Prompt Design Failure Diagnosis

Use this reference after the coverage matrix, quality gate, bias audit, or measurement-readiness evals find a problem.

Each failure diagnosis should produce a repair path. Do not stop at a red flag. If a prompt or prompt set is not ready, provide a revised prompt pattern or a prompt-set adjustment.

## Failure Types

| failure_type | symptom | detection_rule | likely_cause | repair_strategy | revised_prompt_pattern | related_reference_file | quality_gate_result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `branded_overfit` | Most prompts include the client brand, so measurement mostly observes prompted recall. | `brand_included` exceeds the goal-adjusted ratio or branded prompts appear before non-branded discovery prompts. | The prompt set was written from the client's sales narrative instead of buyer search behavior. | Rebalance toward `brand_excluded` discovery, problem-solution, and category prompts; keep branded prompts for validation only. | "What tools or services do companies in [category] compare when they need to [buyer job]?" | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `prompt-quality-rubric.md` | `revise_before_measurement` or `reject` when over-branding is severe. |
| `non_branded_undercoverage` | The set lacks enough natural category, problem, or solution discovery prompts. | `non_branded` is missing or far below the target ratio without a stated diagnosis-goal exception. | The design focuses on brand confirmation instead of market visibility. | Add awareness and exploration prompts with `brand_excluded` and observable category/competitor signals. | "For [persona], what are realistic ways to solve [problem] in [region/category]?" | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `weak_expected_signal` | Prompts exist, but the reason for measuring each prompt is vague or not observable. | `expected_signal` says "check visibility" or "see response quality" without measurable mention, citation, comparison, risk, or recommendation indicators. | The prompt was designed as content ideation, not measurement. | Rewrite expected signals to name observable answer elements. | expected_signal: "Observe whether the answer names the client, which competitor categories appear, and which evaluation axes are used." | `prompt-quality-rubric.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `revise_before_measurement`; `reject` if no diagnostic purpose remains. |
| `duplicate_prompt_cluster` | Multiple prompts ask nearly the same thing with small wording changes. | Same category, persona, buyer stage, intent, and expected signal repeat without a distinct diagnostic reason. | The set was padded to reach a prompt count. | Merge duplicates or vary the category, buyer stage, persona, signal, or competitor rule. | "Split one generic comparison prompt into one category-discovery prompt and one validation-stage evidence prompt." | `prompt-quality-rubric.md`, `prompt-set-iteration-loop.md` | `revise_before_measurement` or `reject` for filler prompts. |
| `buyer_stage_collapse` | Most prompts sit in comparison or decision stages. | Fewer than the mode minimum buyer stages are represented, or stage labels do not match prompt wording. | The design assumes buyers are already vendor-aware. | Add awareness, exploration, validation, and decision prompts with stage-specific language. | "At the awareness stage, what signs tell a [persona] they need a solution for [problem]?" | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `persona_flattening` | Persona labels appear, but prompt wording is the same for every role. | Persona changes do not alter vocabulary, concern, buying task, risk, or expected signal. | Personas were added as labels after the prompt list was written. | Rewrite by role: economic buyer, evaluator, end user, implementer, and executive sponsor where relevant. | "As a [role], what evidence would you look for before shortlisting [category] tools?" | `prompt-quality-rubric.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `revise_before_measurement`; hand off to `recora-persona-discovery` if roles are weak. |
| `competitor_overconstraint` | Comparison prompts force a narrow competitor set or imply the client should win. | Most competitor prompts use `named_competitors` and no `category_competitors` or `unknown_competitor_discovery`. | Known competitors were treated as the full market. | Mix named, category, and unknown competitor discovery; remove winner assumptions. | "Which tools are usually considered alternatives to [category solution] for [buyer role], and why?" | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `prompt-anti-patterns.md` | `revise_before_measurement`; `reject` if leading. |
| `citation_check_missing` | The set cannot show how AI handles sources or evidence. | No `citation_check` category or `evidence_seeking` intent appears when source analysis is part of the goal. | The design focuses only on mentions and recommendations. | Add source-observable prompts and handoff to citation analysis. | "What sources would you use to compare [category] vendors, and what evidence do they provide?" | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `revise_before_measurement` for citation-oriented goals. |
| `seo_keyword_dumping` | A prompt is just keywords or query fragments. | Prompt lacks a natural question, buyer context, or conversational phrasing. | SEO keyword research was pasted into AI-search design. | Convert keywords into realistic buyer questions with stage and expected signal. | Bad: "[keyword] [industry] [region]"; Better: "What should a [persona] compare when choosing [category] tools in [region]?" | `prompt-anti-patterns.md`, `prompt-quality-rubric.md` | `reject` or `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `leading_question_bias` | The prompt asks why the client is best, trusted, cited, or recommended. | Wording presumes a positive outcome or asks AI to validate the client's claim. | Sales copy was turned directly into a prompt. | Replace with neutral evaluation wording and remove outcome assumptions. | "How do buyers evaluate [category] vendors for [use case]?" | `prompt-anti-patterns.md`, `prompt-quality-rubric.md` | `reject` when strongly leading. |
| `measurement_unready` | The prompt cannot be compared across AI engines or parsed into Recora fields. | Multi-question prompt, unclear entity target, provider-specific instruction, missing ID, missing labels, or unobservable signal. | Drafting prioritized human readability over measurement structure. | Split into one measurable question, assign all labels, and confirm extractor-friendly signals. | "prompt_id: [stable_id]; prompt: one buyer-realistic question; expected_signal: named observable elements." | `measurement-readiness-evals.md`, `prompt-set-iteration-loop.md` | `revise_before_measurement` or `reject`. |
| `visibility_without_candidate_opportunity` | Prompt is counted in AI Visibility Rate even though it does not naturally ask for candidate names. | `metric_eligibility.visibility_rate: eligible` while `candidate_mention_opportunity` is `weak` or `none`, or `response_shape` is `evaluation_criteria`, `explanatory_answer`, `evidence_answer`, or `branded_sentiment_answer`. | Brand-excluded prompts were treated as automatically visibility-eligible. | Exclude from visibility or rewrite into a candidate-list / alternative-search / shortlist prompt. | Original: "通販でコーヒー豆を買うとき、価格、容量、レビューはどう確認すべきですか？" Revised: "価格・容量・レビューを比較しやすい通販のコーヒー豆ブランドや商品には、どんな候補がありますか？" | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `prompt-quality-rubric.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `ranking_without_comparable_candidates` | Prompt is counted in AI Ranking even though no ordered or comparable candidate set is expected. | `metric_eligibility.ranking: eligible` while `ranking_opportunity` is `weak` or `none`. | Criteria or checklist prompts were mistaken for ranking prompts. | Exclude from ranking or rewrite to request top candidates, a shortlist, recommended order, or a comparable set. | Original: "高いコーヒー豆と手頃な豆を比べるとき、何を見るべきですか？" Revised: "自宅用に買えるコーヒー豆の候補を、価格帯と特徴が比較できるように紹介してください。" | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `prompt-quality-rubric.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `evaluation_criteria_misclassified_as_visibility` | A "what should I check?" prompt is counted in visibility. | `response_shape: evaluation_criteria` and `visibility_rate: eligible`. | The metric derivation ignored response shape. | Keep it as a criteria/risk prompt, or rewrite to ask for candidate brands/products/services. | "Which [category] candidates should a buyer compare when they care about [criteria]?" | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `evaluation_criteria_misclassified_as_ranking` | A criteria-only prompt is counted in ranking. | `response_shape: evaluation_criteria` and `ranking: eligible`. | The prompt can produce evaluation axes but not candidate order. | Keep ranking excluded, or rewrite to request ranked recommendations or a comparable candidate set. | "Please list candidate [products/vendors/services] in comparable groups by [criteria]." | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `branded_sentiment_meta_question` | Branded sentiment prompt asks about AI behavior instead of using natural buyer wording. | Prompt says "AIはBrandをどう説明しますか？" or "BrandについてAIが説明するとき..." for a buyer-facing sentiment prompt. | The diagnostic intent leaked into prompt wording. | Rewrite as natural brand query while keeping sentiment-only metric eligibility. | Bad: "AIはExample Coffeeをどう説明しますか？" Better: "Example Coffeeの評判は？" | `prompt-quality-rubric.md`, `prompt-set-examples.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `insufficient_branded_sentiment_coverage` | Branded sentiment has too few natural angles to evaluate brand perception. | Sentiment is a goal but fewer than three natural branded queries cover reputation, use-case fit, and concern/risk. | Branded prompts were minimized too aggressively or written as one generic validation prompt. | Add branded sentiment prompts for `overall_reputation`, `use_case_fit`, and `concern_or_risk`; keep all visibility/ranking excluded. | "[Brand]の評判は？"; "[Brand]は[use case]向き？"; "[Brand]を買う前に気をつけることはある？" | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `prompt-set-examples.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `unsupported_market_assumption` | The set assumes market facts, customer segments, competitors, or pricing that were not supplied or verified. | Claims appear in prompts or notes without input support, source evidence, or an explicit `assumption`. | The agent filled context gaps too aggressively. | Mark assumptions, weaken claims, request/handoff missing context, and avoid unsupported competitor or URL analysis. | "Assumption: [category] buyers include [role]. Validate with `recora-persona-discovery` before final measurement." | `public-skill-pattern-research.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `internal_only` until assumptions are validated. |
| `minimum_input_overconfidence` | The output sounds final even though only minimum inputs were provided. | `client_name`, `industry`, `product_or_service`, and `target_customers` are the only supplied fields, but readiness is stated without caveats. | Minimum Input Mode was treated as complete client discovery. | Add Input Completion Table, mark inferred fields, add caveats, and use `production_measurement_ready_with_caveats` instead of unqualified readiness. | "Measurement design is ready with caveats; validate URL, competitors, region, proof, pricing, and claims before client-verified reporting." | `minimum-input-completion.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `revise_before_measurement` or `internal_only` when caveats are missing. |
| `inferred_competitor_hallucination` | Real competitor names appear even though competitors were not supplied. | `competitors` is missing or `needs_confirmation`, but prompts use `named_competitors` or list actual brands. | The agent tried to complete the market map instead of measuring unknown competitor discovery. | Remove invented names, use `unknown_competitor_discovery` or `category_competitors`, and add competitor confirmation to `next_data_needed`. | "Which providers or service categories are commonly compared for [buyer need]?" | `minimum-input-completion.md`, `prompt-coverage-matrix.md` | `reject` for fabricated names; otherwise `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `missing_caveat_for_inferred_context` | Inferred personas, regions, risks, or goals are shown without caveats. | Fields marked or implied as inferred are used in topics/prompts but no `assumptions`, `caveats`, or `needs_confirmation` section exists. | The output optimized for completeness over traceability. | Add source status, confidence, and caveats for each inferred area; downgrade readiness if inference materially affects measurement. | "Assumption: region is inferred from local business model; confirm service area before final measurement." | `minimum-input-completion.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `website_url_absent_overclaim` | The output discusses official pages, schema, pricing page content, or citation readiness without a URL. | `website_url` is missing, but prompts or notes assert site structure, official evidence quality, schema, or crawl/page facts. | The agent inferred website evidence from industry norms. | Remove URL-specific claims, keep prompts as source-observation checks, and mark URL as needed data. | "When evaluating official information, what page types should be checked after the URL is provided?" | `minimum-input-completion.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `revise_before_measurement` or `reject` if the claim is factual. |
| `persona_inference_too_generic` | Personas are inferred but too broad to change prompt wording. | Persona labels such as "user" or "decision maker" appear, but language, stage, and expected signal do not differ by persona. | Target customers were copied into labels without role or concern decomposition. | Infer at least three practical personas from `target_customers`, vary vocabulary and stage, and hand off to `recora-persona-discovery`. | "For a price-sensitive first-time user, what should be checked before choosing [category]?" | `minimum-input-completion.md`, `prompt-quality-rubric.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `diagnosis_goal_too_generic` | The inferred diagnosis goal could apply to any industry. | Goal says only "AI search diagnosis" without category, buyer behavior, comparison, citation, risk, or sentiment targets. | Standard goal was inserted without adapter-specific detail. | Generate a standard goal plus industry-specific modifiers from the adapter, and state it is inferred. | "Diagnose non-branded discovery, comparison, alternative search, citation behavior, and brand sentiment for [industry/category], with [industry-specific risk] caveats." | `minimum-input-completion.md`, `industry-business-model-adapters.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `missing_handoff` | Prompts produce signals that should be analyzed by another Recora skill, but no handoff is assigned. | `handoff_skill` is empty or `none` despite citation, competitor, persona, schema, or client-facing recommendation implications. | Prompt design stopped before downstream analysis planning. | Add one primary handoff per prompt and mention secondary handoffs in `risk_or_bias`. | handoff_skill: "`recora-ai-citation-analysis` for citation/source behavior" | `SKILL.md`, `prompt-set-iteration-loop.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `machine_readability_failure` | The output is useful prose but cannot become rows for measurement. | Missing stable `id`, inconsistent field names, mixed labels, nested prose, or labels outside the allowed values. | The output was written as a narrative deliverable instead of a prompt library. | Normalize field names, allowed labels, stable IDs, and one prompt per row. | Use the `Prompt List` schema from `SKILL.md` and the JSON examples in `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`. | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `vague_topic_set` | Topic names are broad and do not clarify buyer, problem, stage, or diagnostic goal. | `topic_name` or `topic_goal` could apply to many clients and does not connect to categories or signals. | Topics were generated before market category and buyer jobs were defined. | Rewrite topic set around category, buyer job, stage, and observable signal. | topic_name: "[category] vendor discovery for [persona] at [stage]"; topic_goal: "Observe [mention/comparison/citation/risk signal]." | `prompt-coverage-matrix.md`, `prompt-set-examples.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `b2b_template_overapplied_to_b2c` | Consumer, local, product, school, or clinic prompts sound like enterprise SaaS procurement prompts. | B2C/local personas are labeled as executives, procurement, or committees without realistic consumer decision context. | B2B SaaS examples were reused without adapter selection. | Select the correct adapter and rewrite around reviews, price, convenience, location, quality, safety, or first-time concerns. | "What should a first-time user check when choosing [service/product/category] in [context]?" | `industry-business-model-adapters.md`, `prompt-quality-rubric.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `missing_local_intent` | A local business prompt set lacks area, access, opening hours, booking, or nearby comparison angles. | `local_or_regional_coverage` is missing for a local business. | The prompt set treats a local business like a national SaaS/product brand. | Add area/service-area, nearby alternatives, visit/booking, access, review, and availability prompts. | "What should someone compare when choosing a [service] near [area] for [need]?" | `industry-business-model-adapters.md`, `prompt-coverage-matrix.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `missing_consumer_review_angle` | B2C, local, EC, school, or service prompts do not test review/reputation behavior. | `consumer_review_coverage` is missing where reviews naturally influence decisions. | The prompt set overfocuses on category and ignores social proof. | Add review, reputation, price/value, complaint-risk, and first-time concern prompts. | "What review themes should a buyer check before choosing [product/service/category]?" | `industry-business-model-adapters.md`, `measurement-readiness-evals.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `regulated_industry_overclaim` | Healthcare, finance, insurance, law, or real-estate prompts invite diagnosis, legal/financial advice, guaranteed outcomes, or unsupported safety claims. | Prompt asks for what to do medically/legally/financially, or claims safety, returns, cure, compliance, or guaranteed results before evidence. | Regulated industry guardrails were not applied. | Rewrite as general information, comparison criteria, qualification/source verification, risk explanation, or professional-consultation readiness. | "What information should a person verify before consulting a qualified [professional] about [need]?" | `industry-business-model-adapters.md`, `prompt-quality-rubric.md` | `reject` when advice/guarantee framing is strong; otherwise `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `wrong_persona_for_industry` | Persona labels do not match how the industry buys, searches, or decides. | A patient, shopper, parent, student, local visitor, candidate, or investor context is replaced by generic "manager" or "decision maker" language. | Persona was copied from another adapter. | Use adapter persona patterns and rewrite role-specific vocabulary and expected signals. | "As a [realistic persona], what would you check before choosing [category]?" | `industry-business-model-adapters.md`, `prompt-quality-rubric.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |
| `business_model_mismatch` | Prompt families do not match the revenue model, purchase channel, or decision structure. | A subscription, local booking, one-time product purchase, consultation service, or marketplace is measured with the wrong prompt family. | Business model was not mapped before prompt design. | Select primary/secondary adapters and rewrite prompt families, buyer stages, and metric eligibility. | "For a [business model], what alternatives or vendors would a [persona] compare before [decision]?" | `industry-business-model-adapters.md`, `prompt-coverage-matrix.md` | `revise_before_measurement`. |

## Persona Query Realism Failure Types

Use these failures when prompts are safe and structured, but do not sound like the buyer's real AI/search input.

| failure_type | symptom | detection_rule | likely_cause | repair_strategy | bad_prompt | revised_prompt | quality_gate_result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `over_sanitized_query` | Safe and polite, but unlikely to be typed by a real person. | `persona_query_realism` is low or most prompts are long, balanced, and abstract while the persona is anxious, price-sensitive, local, or rushed. | Safety rewriting removed the real user vocabulary. | Keep the user's concern, price/review/local terms, or shorthand, then rewrite into one safe measurable question. | "What general information should a person organize before evaluating cosmetic medical consultation options?" | "美容クリニックが初めてで不安な人は、料金・口コミ・カウンセリング内容をどう確認すればよいですか？" | `revise_before_measurement` |
| `consultant_language_bias` | Prompt sounds like a consulting survey or internal research brief. | Phrases such as "evaluation criteria", "decision framework", or "market positioning" dominate consumer/local prompts. | The prompt was written from analyst language instead of persona language. | Replace formal framing with the words the buyer would use, then keep `expected_signal` structured separately. | "Please organize the comparative decision criteria for selecting a nearby clinic provider." | "近くの美容クリニックを選ぶとき、料金・口コミ・通いやすさはどう比べればいいですか？" | `revise_before_measurement` |
| `missing_raw_search_intent` | Prompt set lacks short search-like or rough query variants. | `language_mode` is only `natural_conversation` or `professional_research` even though the context is BtoC, local, clinic, EC, or urgent service. | The set over-optimized for neat AI-chat questions. | Add `raw_search_like` or `comparison_shortcut` prompts with a safe final wording. | "What information should users compare when considering skin treatment consultation?" | "東京 シミ取り 安い 口コミで調べる人は、料金・口コミ・医師情報・リスク説明をどう確認すべきですか？" | `revise_before_measurement` |
| `missing_emotional_trigger` | BtoC prompts miss anxiety, price, reviews, failure avoidance, or trust concerns. | `target_customers` include first-time, review-heavy, price-sensitive, or safety-conscious users, but prompts avoid those words. | The prompt set treats consumer choices like neutral research. | Add safe `anxious_user` prompts that name uncertainty and verification behavior without making claims. | "What should a user know before visiting a clinic?" | "美容クリニックが初めてで怖い人は、カウンセリング前にどんな説明やリスク情報を確認すると安心材料になりますか？" | `revise_before_measurement` |
| `professional_persona_overgeneralized` | BtoB or specialist prompts do not reflect the role's practical vocabulary. | Every role asks the same generic "compare options" question, with no job-specific tool, workflow, budget, evidence, or pain language. | Persona labels were added after writing generic prompts. | Add role-specific vocabulary and one clear measurable signal. | "What should a manager compare when choosing a business tool?" | "営業管理がExcel運用で限界になった営業責任者は、SFA/CRMを比較するとき何を見ればいいですか？" | `revise_before_measurement` |

## Repair Priority

1. Reject or revise prompts that are leading, unsupported, or machine-unreadable.
2. Restore `non_branded` coverage before adding more branded prompts.
3. Fix weak `expected_signal` values so each prompt has an observable purpose.
4. Remove duplicate clusters before increasing prompt count.
5. Repair persona and buyer-stage coverage.
6. Repair industry/business-model mismatches before increasing prompt count.
7. Add citation and handoff coverage when diagnosis goals require it.
8. Re-run coverage matrix, quality gate, bias audit, and measurement readiness checks.
