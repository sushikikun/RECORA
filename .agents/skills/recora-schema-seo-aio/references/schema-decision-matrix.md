# Schema Decision Matrix

Research date: 2026-06-21

Purpose: decide when schema is appropriate for Recora/client pages. Schema is a support layer. It does not guarantee AI citation, rich results, ranking improvement, traffic, or conversion.

## Hard Rules

- Do not put information in schema that is absent from visible or clearly supportable page content.
- Review and AggregateRating are allowed only when reviews are real, visible or legitimately represented, and the aggregation basis can be explained.
- FAQPage is allowed only when visible page FAQ text matches the JSON-LD.
- Validate rendered output, not only source snippets, when implementation may be JS-rendered.
- Separate schema.org type validity from Google rich-result eligibility.

## Matrix

| Schema | When to consider | Required visible content | When not to use | Risk | Validation method | Recora-safe wording | Bad recommendation | Good recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Organization | LP, about, publisher, provider identity pages. | Official name, URL, logo, brand/entity facts, contact/profile links if used. | To imply authority, reviews, or third-party validation. | Invented sameAs/contact/legal facts. | JSON-LD parse, visible entity match, official profile check. | "Organization schema can clarify entity identity." | "Add Organization schema to look authoritative." | "Add Organization schema only for visible official entity facts and profiles." |
| SoftwareApplication | Recora/software product pages. | App/product name, provider, category, features, operating context, offer/pricing status if used. | Service-only pages, vague product pages, missing visible feature/offer facts. | Invented features, prices, ratings, OS/platform. | Rendered JSON-LD, body/schema consistency, schema validator. | "May support product interpretation when visible facts exist." | "SoftwareApplication schema will get AI citations." | "Use SoftwareApplication where the LP visibly states product category, features, and provider." |
| Service | Diagnosis, consulting, implementation, or managed service pages. | Provider, service type, audience, deliverables, process, area/channel if used. | Product pages where software is primary or service facts are vague. | Invented service areas or offers. | Body/schema match, page intent review. | "Service schema can describe the visible service offer." | "Add Service schema to rank for services." | "Use Service schema after adding visible deliverables and provider facts." |
| Product | Product/SaaS offer pages where product framing is visible. | Product name, brand/provider, description, offer facts if used. | Pure service/consulting pages; no product facts; no visible offer. | Product/Service ambiguity, fake offers/ratings. | Schema parse, offer/review field check, visible content check. | "Product schema is optional and depends on page intent." | "Use Product schema on every pricing page." | "Consider Product only when the page visibly presents Recora as a product offer." |
| FAQPage | FAQ page or section. | Visible question/answer pairs that match JSON-LD. | Hidden/incomplete Q&A, sales claims disguised as FAQ, no visible FAQ. | Mismatch, outdated Google feature expectations. | Extract visible Q&A, compare JSON-LD, validate rendered page. | "FAQPage schema may support interpretation where visible Q&A exists." | "FAQ schema makes ChatGPT cite the page." | "Add FAQPage JSON-LD only after visible one-question-one-answer FAQ exists." |
| Article | Glossary, research, public report, guide, case study narrative. | Headline, body, author/publisher where available, date/update date if relevant. | Thin landing pages, product pages, pages without article-like body. | Invented author/date, weak content treated as research. | Body/headline/date check, rendered JSON-LD parse. | "Article schema can clarify report or glossary content." | "Add Article schema to product pages for SEO." | "Use Article for public reports with visible methodology and update date." |
| BreadcrumbList | Pages with meaningful hierarchy. | Visible or clearly supported breadcrumb/site hierarchy with ordered items. | Flat pages with no hierarchy, invented navigation. | Misleading hierarchy. | Compare breadcrumb JSON-LD to visible nav/internal structure. | "BreadcrumbList can clarify page hierarchy." | "Add breadcrumb schema to every page even without breadcrumbs." | "Use BreadcrumbList when the hierarchy is visible or reflected in site structure." |
| Review | Pages with individual real reviews/testimonials. | Reviewer/source, review body or summary, date if used, visible/testimonial evidence. | Fabricated, private, unverifiable, or non-visible reviews. | Trust damage, policy risk, false claims. | Verify review visibility/source/permission and schema fields. | "Review schema is only considered for real, supportable review content." | "Add Review schema to create trust." | "Do not propose Review schema unless real visible reviews exist and approval is clear." |
| AggregateRating | Pages with real aggregated ratings and explainable basis. | Rating value, count, source/basis, visible or legitimately represented aggregate. | No reviews, vague testimonials, internal invented score, unverifiable source. | Severe overclaiming and misleading markup. | Check rating source, count, visibility, aggregation method, schema policy. | "AggregateRating is high-risk and evidence-dependent." | "Add 5-star AggregateRating to improve snippets." | "Reject AggregateRating unless the page has real, visible, explainable review aggregation." |
