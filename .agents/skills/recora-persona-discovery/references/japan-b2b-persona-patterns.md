# Japan B2B Persona Patterns

Use this reference when the target is Japan, Japanese-language, B2B, SEO/GEO/AIO/LLMO, agency, consultant, or unclear but likely B2B.

These are patterns, not facts about any specific brand. Confirm them from site evidence or customer data before increasing confidence.

## Japan B2B Decision Context

Important buying and search patterns:

- `稟議`: Internal approval often needs budget reason, risk reduction, implementation plan, comparison table, and proof.
- `無料診断`: A low-friction CTA, but buyers may question reliability, scope, data required, and whether sales pressure follows.
- `資料請求`: Often signals evaluation-stage intent and need for internal sharing.
- `導入事例`: Buyers often need same-industry, same-size, or same-problem examples.
- Price opacity: "contact us" or quote-based pricing increases questions about budget, ROI, and hidden cost.
- Support structure: Japanese-language support, onboarding, reporting cadence, and post-diagnosis next steps can matter.
- Existing SEO difference: Users may ask how AI search/GEO/AIO/LLMO differs from current SEO, content, or PR work.
- GEO/AIO/LLMO literacy gap: Some personas need basic category education; others need methodology, measurement, or evidence rigor.
- Agency/consultant involvement: Agencies may evaluate client-reporting value, resale fit, white-label boundaries, and operational burden.

## Common Japan B2B Persona Patterns

### Executive sponsor / business owner

- Role fit: `decision_maker`
- Search intent: Understand business impact, competitive risk, and whether AI-search visibility is worth budget.
- Comparison axis: ROI, strategic urgency, competitor risk, credibility, implementation burden.
- Concerns: Unclear value, immature category, unmeasured results, internal approval difficulty.
- Needs to see: Executive summary, diagnosis sample, competitor examples, pricing logic, next-step roadmap.
- Prompt angle cue: Test prompts where an executive compares AI-search diagnosis with existing SEO or brand/PR initiatives and asks what proof justifies budget.

### Marketing manager / SEO owner

- Role fit: `end_user` or `evaluator`
- Search intent: Learn what to measure, how diagnosis works, and what actions follow.
- Comparison axis: Methodology, prompt coverage, citation analysis, reporting clarity, actionability.
- Concerns: Generic SEO advice, black-box scoring, unclear evidence, duplicate work with current SEO tools.
- Needs to see: Diagnosis methodology, sample prompt categories, evidence rules, output sample, implementation examples.
- Prompt angle cue: Test prompts where a marketing or SEO owner compares AI-search diagnosis methods and checks how GEO/AIO/LLMO differs from current SEO.

### Department head / economic buyer

- Role fit: `economic_buyer`
- Search intent: Estimate budget, internal cost, impact, and whether a free diagnosis is enough.
- Comparison axis: Price, time-to-value, reporting cadence, vendor support, expected deliverables.
- Concerns: Pricing opacity, unclear scope, extra consulting cost, no clear ownership after diagnosis.
- Needs to see: Scope, pricing explanation, plan tiers or quote logic, sample deliverables, recommended operating model.
- Prompt angle cue: Test prompts where an economic buyer asks what budget and evidence are needed before requesting an AI-search diagnosis.

### Sales enablement or product marketing lead

- Role fit: `influencer`
- Search intent: Improve sales materials, comparison pages, objection handling, and category explanations.
- Comparison axis: Competitor framing, evidence clarity, buyer objections, content gaps.
- Concerns: Diagnosis output may not translate into sales assets or content improvements.
- Needs to see: Recommendations linked to landing pages, comparison pages, case studies, FAQs, and sales talk tracks.
- Prompt angle cue: Test prompts where a product marketing or sales enablement lead asks how AI answers compare vendors and what proof buyers need.

### Agency / consultant evaluator

- Role fit: `agency_or_consultant`
- Search intent: Decide whether the service helps client reporting, audits, or strategy.
- Comparison axis: Client-facing report quality, repeatability, white-label fit, scope boundaries, evidence rigor.
- Concerns: Overlap with existing SEO consulting, client confusion, unsupported AI visibility claims, operational burden.
- Needs to see: Client-safe language, sample report, evidence policy, handoff workflow, limitations.
- Prompt angle cue: Test prompts where an agency compares AI-search diagnosis services for client reporting and checks methodology, evidence, and resale fit.

## Japan B2B Verification Questions

Ask or mark as `needs_verification`:

- Is there evidence that the buyer must prepare internal approval materials?
- Does the site offer free diagnosis, documents, demo, consultation, or quote request?
- Are pricing details explicit or opaque?
- Are case studies available and specific enough?
- Does the site explain how AI-search diagnosis differs from traditional SEO?
- Does the site mention Japanese support, onboarding, reporting, or consulting?
- Are agencies or consultants explicitly targeted?
