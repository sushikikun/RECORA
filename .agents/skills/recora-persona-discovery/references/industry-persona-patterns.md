# Industry Persona Patterns

Use this reference after `business-type-router.md` to choose industry-specific persona patterns.

These are pattern hypotheses, not claims about a specific brand. Use site evidence before including any persona.

## Required Output Shape

```md
| industry_pattern | typical_persona_roles | buyer_user_comparator_influencer_separation | common_pains | trigger_events | comparison_criteria | trust_requirements | objections | vocabulary | alternatives_considered | prompt_angle_examples | unsupported_persona_examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
```

| Local store / local service | purchaser, user, family_decision_maker, comparator, repeat_user, recommender | Local chooser, payer, service recipient, and family recommender may differ. | trust, convenience, availability, price, service quality | urgent need, event, bad experience, recommendation, location change | location, reviews, price, service scope, availability, staff | reviews, photos, staff, pricing, booking/contact flow | "Can I trust them?"; "Are they nearby?"; "What will it cost?" | near me, reviews, reservation, price, open hours, staff | nearby competitor, chain store, DIY, wait | Test prompts where a local comparator checks nearby options, reviews, price, availability, and trust before booking. | "local residents" only |

## Use Rules

- Use patterns to generate hypotheses, not to assert real customers.
- If regulated risk exists, also apply `regulated-industry-cautions.md`.
- If local trust matters, also apply `local-trust-service-personas.md`.
- Exclude persona rows that do not create a distinct prompt angle.
