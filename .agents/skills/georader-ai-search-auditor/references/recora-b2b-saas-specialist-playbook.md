# Recora BtoB SaaS Specialist Playbook

Use this playbook when creating Recora or GEORADER GEO/SEO/AI search strategy for BtoB SaaS companies. It combines official-source boundaries, research-backed concepts, industry practice, and Recora assumptions into practical audit and roadmap guidance.

This playbook is not an official standard. Label Recora scoring, prioritization, and diagnosis structure as `GEORADER_ASSUMPTION`. Mark client-specific facts, current competitor visibility, search demand, SERP state, AI answer visibility, and conversion performance as `NEEDS_VERIFICATION` unless supplied or measured.

## BtoB SaaS Buyer Journey Query Map

Map queries across:

- Awareness: problem education, market change, risk, inefficiency, compliance pressure.
- Problem-aware: "how to solve", "reduce", "automate", "improve", "avoid", "measure".
- Category-aware: software category, tool type, platform class, managed service category.
- Comparison: vendor A vs vendor B, best tools, shortlist, evaluation criteria.
- Alternative: alternative to known vendor, replacement, migration, open-source alternative.
- Pricing: price, cost, ROI, calculator, budget, total cost of ownership.
- Proof: case study, results, examples, benchmark, reviews, testimonials.
- Security: security, privacy, compliance, data handling, SOC2, SSO, permissions.
- Implementation: onboarding, setup, migration, timeline, training, operations.
- Integration: CRM, MA, BI, data warehouse, Slack, Teams, API, workflow tools.
- ROI: savings, productivity, revenue impact, risk reduction, payback period.
- Vendor shortlist: recommended tools, top vendors, best for industry or use case.

## Recora Diagnostic Query System

Default query mix:

- 60% non-branded discovery.
- 20% comparison / alternative.
- 10% branded / entity verification.
- 10% citation / evidence verification.

Use this as a Recora internal framework, not as an official platform rule.

## Content Asset Map

Prioritize assets by buyer risk and evidence needs:

- Category hub: define the category and buying criteria.
- Problem pages: map pains to use cases and evidence.
- Use-case pages: show workflows, users, outcomes, and proof.
- Industry pages: show domain language and examples.
- Comparison pages: clarify tradeoffs without fake superiority claims.
- Alternative pages: explain migration and fit.
- Pricing / ROI pages: reduce budget ambiguity.
- Methodology: explain how diagnosis, scoring, or recommendations are made.
- Case studies: provide concrete proof and measured outcomes only when verified.
- Security / trust pages: answer procurement and risk questions.
- Integration pages: connect product value to existing systems.
- FAQ: answer high-intent buyer questions.
- Glossary: define category and entity concepts.
- Sample report: show output quality and next actions.

## Entity And Trust Assets

Review and recommend:

- company page.
- product page.
- founder or expert page.
- author and reviewer information.
- external profiles.
- reviews and directory listings.
- press and mentions.
- partner pages.
- consistent naming across owned and third-party sources.

## Measurement Fields

Track:

- AI answer mention.
- recommendation status.
- citation URLs.
- source type.
- competitor mentions.
- SERP position.
- query fan-out cluster.
- evidence gap.
- conversion path.
- platform, date, location, and prompt variant when available.

## P0 / P1 / P2 Examples

`P0`:

- Client-facing report claims AI visibility, citation, ranking, delivery, or competitor status without evidence.
- Critical pages appear blocked from crawling or indexing, but the report presents AI absence as a content-only issue.
- Form, report, payment, or diagnosis flow implies completion without verified backend behavior.
- Secret access is requested for audit evidence.

`P1`:

- Category and problem pages do not define the product clearly enough for non-branded discovery.
- Comparison and alternative pages are missing while competitors dominate shortlist queries.
- Proof assets are weak: no case studies, methodology, security, integration, or ROI pages.
- Entity signals are inconsistent across owned pages and third-party profiles.
- SERP visibility and AI answer visibility are not measured separately.

`P2`:

- FAQ and glossary coverage can be expanded after core category, proof, and comparison assets are fixed.
- Structured data opportunities exist but visible content needs refinement first.
- Report wording can be made more client-safe and evidence-scoped.
- Monthly monitoring can add query clusters, source overlap, and answer consistency tracking.

## Client-Facing Safe Wording

- "The goal is to improve discoverability, source clarity, and citation readiness, not to guarantee AI recommendation."
- "Current AI answer visibility and competitor presence should be measured before they are stated as confirmed findings."
- "Recora separates traditional search visibility from AI answer visibility because the source sets and recommendation behavior can differ."
- "The recommendations are prioritized by expected impact, evidence strength, and buyer decision risk."
