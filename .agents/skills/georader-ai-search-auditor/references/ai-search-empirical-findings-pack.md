# AI Search Empirical Findings Pack

Use this pack when Recora or GEORADER outputs discuss empirical AI search studies, AI Overviews, Gemini, traditional Google results, source overlap, citation behavior, answer consistency, or competitor visibility measurement.

This pack is not official platform policy. Use empirical studies as `RESEARCH_BACKED` only for what the studies actually observed. Treat current competitor visibility as `NEEDS_VERIFICATION` unless measured for the target, query set, date, platform, and location.

## Source Targets To Verify

- How Generative AI Disrupts Search: An Empirical Study of Google Search, Gemini, and AI Overviews.
- Reputable empirical studies about AI Overviews, citations, source overlap, or AI search behavior.
- Cloudflare / Perplexity crawler controversy only as industry uncertainty, not as settled official policy.

Do not cite these source targets as verified unless the paper, article, or source was inspected during the task or supplied by the user.

## Safe Findings To Use

- Empirical studies can suggest that generative search and traditional search retrieve or cite different source sets.
- AI Overviews, Gemini, and traditional Google results may differ in source overlap and consistency when studies observed that pattern.
- These studies support Recora measuring SERP visibility and AI answer visibility separately.
- Third-party studies can inform measurement design, but they are not official platform policy.
- Current competitor visibility, citation share, answer sentiment, and recommendation status need fresh measurement for the target.

## Measurement Implications For Recora

Recora reports should separate:

- traditional SERP visibility.
- AI answer mention.
- AI answer recommendation status.
- citation URLs.
- source type and authority.
- competitor mentions.
- answer consistency across prompt variants.
- date, platform, location, and account state when known.

This separation helps avoid assuming that ranking in classic SERPs automatically means inclusion in AI answers, or that AI citation automatically means strong SEO ranking.

## Evidence Labels

- `RESEARCH_BACKED`: Named empirical study supports a general pattern under its study design.
- `CONFIRMED_FACT`: Supplied AI answer exports, SERP exports, screenshots, or measured query results were inspected.
- `INDUSTRY_PRACTICE`: Separating SERP and AI visibility is a prudent measurement practice.
- `GEORADER_ASSUMPTION`: Recora uses the separation as a report and prioritization framework.
- `NEEDS_VERIFICATION`: Current target visibility, competitor visibility, citation overlap, source freshness, platform behavior, or performance impact.

## Do Not Claim

- Do not treat third-party studies as official Google, OpenAI, Anthropic, or Perplexity policy.
- Do not claim a study proves current results for a specific client.
- Do not claim Cloudflare / Perplexity controversy proves all crawler behavior or all platform compliance.
- Do not invent competitor visibility or AI answer results.
- Do not guarantee lift, citation, ranking, traffic, or conversion.

## Client-Facing Safe Language

- "Research suggests AI answer visibility and traditional search visibility can differ, so Recora measures them separately."
- "Current competitor visibility should be confirmed with fresh query tests before being presented as a finding."
- "The report distinguishes observed results from research-informed hypotheses and next verification steps."
