# Official Google AI SEO Research Pack

Use this pack when Recora or GEORADER outputs discuss Google Search, AI Overviews, AI Mode, generative AI features on Google Search, crawlability, indexing, snippets, or SEO fundamentals.

This pack is a research guide, not proof by itself. Treat the source targets as verification targets. Assign `OFFICIAL_SOURCE` only to claims explicitly supported by current Google-owned documentation inspected during the task or supplied by the user. If the exact source URL, current source status, or last checked date is unknown, mark the affected claim `NEEDS_VERIFICATION`.

Do not use this local pack as a client-facing citation. Cite or summarize the original Google source only when known and verified.

## Safe Claim Boundaries

- Google Search generative AI features should be treated as part of SEO and Search visibility, not as a separate magic channel.
- SEO fundamentals remain relevant for visibility in Google Search features, including AI Overviews and AI Mode, when Google documentation explicitly supports that wording.
- Google AI features may use query fan-out across subtopics and data sources when Google describes that behavior.
- To be eligible as supporting links in AI Overviews or AI Mode, pages need to be indexed and eligible to show snippets in Google Search when current Google guidance supports that claim.
- Google does not guarantee crawling, indexing, serving, AI appearance, ranking, traffic, or conversion.
- Google guidance is `OFFICIAL_SOURCE` only for what Google explicitly says.

## Do Not Claim

- Do not claim Google says FAQ sections, schema markup, crawler changes, or comparison tables guarantee AI citation.
- Do not claim special GEO markup is required or officially endorsed unless a current Google source explicitly says so.
- Do not claim Recora's framework is a Google standard.
- Do not claim query fan-out creates guaranteed ranking, citation, or recommendation outcomes.
- Do not invent last checked dates, last updated dates, URLs, or page titles.
