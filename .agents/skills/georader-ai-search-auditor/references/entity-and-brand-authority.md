# Entity And Brand Authority

Use this reference when analyzing whether AI systems and search engines can understand who the brand is, what it does, and why it should be trusted.

## Evidence Label

- Entity clarity checks from inspected pages are `CONFIRMED_FACT`.
- Recommendations about third-party presence and proof are usually `INDUSTRY_PRACTICE`.
- GEORADER packaging and product assumptions are `GEORADER_ASSUMPTION`.

## Entity Clarity Checks

Check whether public pages clearly state:

- company or service name.
- domain and canonical brand spelling.
- what the service does.
- who it is for and not for.
- country/region served.
- operator/company information.
- contact path.
- author or reviewer identity for educational content.
- pricing, package, or consultation model when relevant.
- sameAs/social/profile links when present and accurate.

## Authority Signals

Look for:

- case studies with measurable outcomes.
- customer logos or testimonials that are real and approved.
- methodology pages.
- sample reports.
- named experts or authors.
- third-party mentions, press, partner profiles, directories, and review platforms.
- consistent descriptions across owned and third-party sources.

## GEORADER-Specific Review

For GEORADER, ensure the entity is not just "AI marketing" but specifically:

- AI search visibility diagnosis.
- competitor AI answer comparison.
- citation/source readiness analysis.
- report and improvement roadmap.
- ongoing monitoring and monthly optimization.

## Output Pattern

| Signal | Current evidence | Risk | Recommended improvement | Evidence label |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Do not invent external mentions, customers, testimonials, or case studies. Mark missing proof as an opportunity.
