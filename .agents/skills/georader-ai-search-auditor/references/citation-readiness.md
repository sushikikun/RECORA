# Citation Readiness

Use this reference when checking whether a site gives AI systems enough accessible evidence to cite, summarize, or recommend it.

## Evidence Rule

Citation readiness is not the same as guaranteed citation. Treat recommendations as `INDUSTRY_PRACTICE` or `GEORADER_ASSUMPTION` unless actual AI answer outputs, cited URLs, official guidance, or research evidence are supplied.

## Core Checks

Check for:

- clear service description.
- who the service is for and not for.
- pricing, price range, package, or consultation model.
- methodology.
- examples or sample report.
- case studies.
- testimonials or customer proof.
- FAQ.
- glossary/definition pages.
- comparison pages.
- use-case pages.
- industry pages.
- region pages when local discovery matters.
- company profile, operator identity, contact, and author information.
- crawlable text instead of image-only claims.
- structured headings.
- structured data opportunities that match visible content.
- external citations, directory listings, press, partner pages, review profiles, or independent mentions.

## GEORADER-Specific Content Gaps

Watch for missing pages or sections explaining:

- GEOとは.
- AI検索最適化とは.
- SEOとGEOの違い.
- ChatGPTで自社名が出ない理由.
- AI検索で競合だけが出る理由.
- GEO診断レポートの見方.
- 業界別AI検索対策.
- ChatGPT / Gemini / Perplexity 別の見え方.
- 改善前後の事例.
- 代理店向け販売資料.

## Readiness Levels

- `low`: vague service claims, little proof, thin crawlable text, no external evidence.
- `medium`: service and target are clear, but proof, examples, structured evidence, and citations are incomplete.
- `high`: clear entity, strong proof, detailed methodology, report examples, external references, and citation-ready pages.

## Output Rules

Tie each gap to an AI-search reason:

- "AI cannot confidently recommend this because..."
- "AI has no citation-ready source for..."
- "Competitors may win because they publish..."

Never say the fix guarantees AI citation or recommendation.
