# AI Citation Strategy

Use this reference for AI search visibility, source readiness, and citation-oriented recommendations.

## Evidence Labels

- Supplied AI answers and cited URLs are `CONFIRMED_FACT`.
- Platform documentation is `OFFICIAL_SOURCE` only when verified from the platform.
- Academic or credible research findings are `RESEARCH_BACKED` when named and scoped.
- Content structure patterns are usually `INDUSTRY_PRACTICE`.
- GEORADER scoring and report packaging are `GEORADER_ASSUMPTION`.

## Citation Readiness Checks

For each important claim, ask:

- Is the claim visible in crawlable text?
- Is there a page that directly supports it?
- Is the page specific enough to be cited without surrounding context?
- Does the page include date, author/company, methodology, examples, or proof?
- Are there external sources that support the brand/entity?
- Are competitors providing clearer evidence?
- Does the AI answer cite sources that actually support the claim?

## Content Patterns

Use these as industry-practice patterns, not official standards:

- definition blocks for "what is" queries.
- comparison tables for "A vs B" queries.
- FAQ blocks for natural-language questions.
- methodology sections for diagnostic services.
- case-study summaries for proof.
- source-backed statistic or evidence blocks when real sources exist.
- sample report excerpts when clearly labeled as samples.

## Source Strategy

Prioritize:

1. owned pages that explain service, methodology, pricing/package, examples, and proof.
2. third-party profiles, directories, press, partner pages, and review sites where accurate.
3. report assets that clients can share internally.
4. clear citation trails from claim to source.

## Output Pattern

| Claim / query | Current source readiness | Missing evidence | Recommended asset | Evidence label |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Do not guarantee citation. Say the work improves citation readiness and must be tested with fresh AI answer queries.
