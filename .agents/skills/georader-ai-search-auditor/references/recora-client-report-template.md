# Recora Client Report Template

Use this reference to create a Recora client report that is evidence-scoped, source-grounded, and safe for AI search, GEO, SEO, and citation-readiness work.

## Report Structure

### Scope And Evidence

State the target, date, output type, inspected materials, official sources inspected, research sources inspected, user-provided facts, Recora or GEORADER assumptions, needs-verification items, and not-inspected areas.

### Executive Summary

Summarize only what the evidence supports. Separate:

- confirmed visibility or citation evidence
- official-source-backed access or eligibility guidance
- research-backed context
- industry-practice recommendations
- Recora / GEORADER assumptions
- current unknowns

### Evidence Ledger

Use a table with these columns:

| Claim | Evidence label | Basis | Client-safe wording | Verification needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Every important claim about AI visibility, crawler access, technical access, citation readiness, competitors, rankings, traffic, conversion, business impact, or Recora product capability must appear in the ledger or be clearly labeled inline.

### Findings

Lead with P0/P1/P2 findings.

For each finding include:

- severity
- issue
- evidence label
- evidence or assumption
- why it matters
- recommended action
- owner or area when known
- client-safe status

### AI Platform Access

Cover crawler and access readiness without guarantees:

- Google Search and AI feature eligibility signals
- OpenAI crawler access scope
- Anthropic crawler access scope
- Perplexity crawler access scope
- robots.txt and CDN/WAF constraints
- visible text, renderability, and source accessibility
- query-level testing needed before claims

### Source And Citation Readiness

Assess whether content gives AI/search systems citable evidence:

- clear entity and brand facts
- first-party proof
- third-party proof
- methodology and data provenance
- comparison and alternatives pages
- FAQ, glossary, industry, case-study, and proof assets
- author/company trust signals
- structured data opportunities that match visible content

### Competitor And Query Evidence

Separate supplied evidence from assumptions:

- non-branded discovery queries
- comparison queries
- problem-aware queries
- branded verification queries
- cited domains and competitor mentions from supplied AI answers
- missing fresh tests

### Roadmap

Group recommendations by:

- P0 trust, safety, source, or implementation-risk blockers
- P1 high-impact visibility, content, technical, report, or conversion improvements
- P2 refinements, experiments, and monthly monitoring

### Recora Scope Note

End with a short note when Recora facts are not independently verified:

"Recora is used here as the report/service framing. Product capabilities, implementation status, platform visibility, and business claims should be treated as `NEEDS_VERIFICATION` unless separately confirmed."

## Client-Safe Language

Use:

- "improves citation readiness"
- "supports crawler access under documented platform rules"
- "requires fresh AI answer testing"
- "based on Recora's internal evidence-scope framework"

Avoid:

- "guarantees citation"
- "official GEO standard"
- "AI systems will recommend this"
- "crawler access proves visibility"
- "Recora has implemented this" without verified evidence
