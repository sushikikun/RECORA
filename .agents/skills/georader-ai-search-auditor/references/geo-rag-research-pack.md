# GEO And RAG Research Pack

Use this pack when Recora or GEORADER outputs discuss generative engine optimization, LLM visibility, source inclusion, generated answer attribution, retrieval-augmented generation, or research-backed AI search concepts.

This pack supports research literacy. It is not official platform guidance and does not prove current behavior for Google, OpenAI, Anthropic, Perplexity, Gemini, ChatGPT, Claude, or any commercial AI search product.

## Source Targets To Verify

- GEO: Generative Engine Optimization, arXiv 2311.09735.
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks, arXiv 2005.11401.
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation survey papers when useful.
- AgenticGEO as emerging research only, not production guidance.

Use `RESEARCH_BACKED` only when a named paper and the supported claim are known. If source details or current relevance are unknown, use `NEEDS_VERIFICATION`.

## GEO Research Boundaries

- The GEO paper frames generative engines as synthesizing answers from multiple sources and introduces visibility-oriented optimization.
- GEO research can support the idea of measuring visibility, attribution, and source inclusion in generated answers.
- The "up to 40%" visibility improvement is a research result under specific experimental conditions, not a commercial guarantee.
- GEO research does not make Recora an official standard.
- GEO research does not prove that a tactic will work for every client, query, industry, or AI platform.

## RAG Research Boundaries

- RAG research supports the general concept that language model systems can combine parametric models with retrieved external information.
- RAG can support thinking about source retrievability, grounding, and provenance.
- RAG does not prove that every AI search platform retrieves, ranks, grounds, cites, or summarizes sources in the same way.
- RAG research does not prove current behavior for AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, or other commercial systems unless current platform evidence also supports the claim.

## Safe Labels

- `RESEARCH_BACKED`: A named paper supports the concept or studied behavior.
- `INDUSTRY_PRACTICE`: Common GEO/SEO measurement practice inspired by research and market behavior.
- `GEORADER_ASSUMPTION`: Recora uses research concepts to design its evidence-scope framework, scoring, or roadmap.
- `NEEDS_VERIFICATION`: Current platform behavior, commercial outcomes, client-specific gains, competitor visibility, citation rates, or performance lift.

## Unsafe Wording

- "GEO research proves Recora clients will improve visibility by 40%."
- "RAG means all AI search engines cite the same sources."
- "This is an official AI search standard."
- "Research proves Google, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Perplexity behave the same way."

## Recora Strategy Use

Use GEO and RAG research to justify measuring:

- query-level AI answer presence.
- brand mention and recommendation status.
- citation URLs and source types.
- source inclusion and attribution patterns.
- answer consistency across prompts and platforms.
- evidence gaps compared with competitors.

Keep the recommendations practical:

- verify current AI answer behavior.
- compare SERP visibility and AI answer visibility separately.
- improve source clarity and extractability.
- build evidence-rich assets before claiming outcomes.

## Client-Facing Safe Language

- "Research supports measuring visibility and source inclusion in generated answers, but current platform behavior and client-specific results must be verified."
- "Recora uses research-informed methods as an internal evidence framework, not as an official platform standard."
- "The goal is to improve citation readiness and measurement quality, not to guarantee AI citation."
